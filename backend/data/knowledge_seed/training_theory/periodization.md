# Periodization

## Definition

Periodization is the systematic planning of athletic training. It involves progressive cycling of various aspects of a training program during a specific time period.

## Models

### Linear Periodization
- Gradual increase in intensity with decrease in volume over a macrocycle.
- Example: 4 weeks hypertrophy (3x10) -> 4 weeks strength (4x6) -> 4 weeks power (5x3)
- Best for beginners who need consistent progression.

### Undulating Periodization (DUP)
- Intensity and volume vary within a week.
- Example: Monday heavy (5x3), Wednesday moderate (4x8), Friday light (3x12)
- Allows more frequent exposure to different training stimuli.

### Block Periodization
- Each block (3-6 weeks) focuses on one primary quality.
- Example: Accumulation block -> Transmutation block -> Realization block
- Best for advanced athletes with specific competition goals.

## Periodization for Squat Training

| Phase | Duration | Sets x Reps | Intensity (%1RM) | Focus |
|-------|----------|-------------|-------------------|-------|
| Hypertrophy | 3-4 weeks | 3-4 x 8-12 | 65-75% | Muscle growth, work capacity |
| Strength | 3-4 weeks | 4-5 x 3-6 | 80-90% | Neural adaptations, maximal force |
| Peaking | 2-3 weeks | 3-5 x 1-3 | 90-100% | Competition preparation |
| Deload | 1 week | 2-3 x 5-8 | 60-70% | Recovery, technique refinement |
