# Progressive Overload

## Principle

Progressive overload is the gradual increase of stress placed on the musculoskeletal system during exercise training. It is the foundational principle of all strength and hypertrophy programs.

## Methods of Progression

### 1. Load (Weight)
- Increase by 2.5-5 kg for upper body lifts per session
- Increase by 5-10 kg for lower body lifts per session
- Microplates (0.5-1.25 kg) are useful for smaller increments

### 2. Volume (Sets x Reps)
- Add 1 set per muscle group per week (up to a practical limit)
- Increase reps within a rep range before adding weight
- Example: 3x8 -> 3x10 -> 3x12 -> add weight, return to 3x8

### 3. Frequency
- Training a muscle group 2-3x per week generally produces superior results to 1x per week.
- Higher frequency allows more total volume distribution.

### 4. Density
- Reduce rest periods while maintaining the same load and reps
- Increases metabolic stress and time efficiency

### 5. Range of Motion
- Increase exercise ROM as mobility improves
- Example: partial squat -> parallel squat -> deep squat

## Autoregulation

RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion) scale:
- RPE 6-7: Warm-up / technique work
- RPE 7-8: Working sets (2-3 reps in reserve)
- RPE 8-9: Challenging sets (1-2 reps in reserve)
- RPE 10: Maximum effort / failure

## Deloading

- Reduce volume or intensity by 40-60% every 4-6 weeks
- Allows connective tissue recovery and CNS restoration
- Prevents accumulated fatigue from impairing progress
