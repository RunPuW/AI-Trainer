# Common Squat-Related Injuries and Prevention

## Knee Injuries

### Patellofemoral Pain Syndrome (PFPS)
- **Symptom**: Anterior knee pain around or behind the patella
- **Cause**: Excessive valgus, poor patellar tracking, overuse
- **Prevention**: Strengthen VMO and hip abductors, avoid excessive valgus

### Meniscus Tears
- **Symptom**: Medial or lateral joint line pain, clicking, locking
- **Cause**: Excessive rotational forces under load, deep squat with poor control
- **Prevention**: Proper warm-up, controlled descent speed, avoid twisting under load

### ACL/PCL Strain
- **Symptom**: Acute pain, swelling, instability
- **Cause**: Sudden valgus collapse, hyperextension
- **Prevention**: Neuromuscular training, proper landing mechanics

## Lower Back Injuries

### Lumbar Disc Herniation
- **Symptom**: Low back pain radiating to leg, numbness, tingling
- **Cause**: Excessive lumbar flexion under load ("butt wink")
- **Prevention**: Maintain neutral spine, improve hip mobility, appropriate depth

### Lumbar Strain
- **Symptom**: Localized low back pain, muscle spasm
- **Cause**: Rounding of the lower back, excessive forward lean
- **Prevention**: Core bracing, proper breathing (Valsalva maneuver)

## Hip Injuries

### Hip Flexor Strain
- **Symptom**: Anterior hip pain, pain with hip extension
- **Cause**: Excessive hip flexor tightness, aggressive hip hinge
- **Prevention**: Hip flexor stretching, gradual ROM increases

### Labral Tear
- **Symptom**: Deep groin pain, clicking, catching
- **Cause**: Excessive hip flexion with internal rotation
- **Prevention**: Avoid "knees caving in" at depth, appropriate squat depth

## Shoulder Injuries

### Shoulder Impingement
- **Symptom**: Pain with overhead activities, anterior shoulder pain
- **Cause**: Excessive shoulder external rotation in low-bar squat
- **Prevention**: Proper bar placement, shoulder mobility work

## General Prevention Strategies

1. **Warm-up**: 5-10 minutes of general warm-up + specific movement prep
2. **Progressive loading**: Increase weight by no more than 10% per week
3. **Form before load**: Master bodyweight squat before adding external load
4. **Adequate recovery**: 48-72 hours between heavy squat sessions
5. **Mobility maintenance**: Regular stretching and foam rolling
